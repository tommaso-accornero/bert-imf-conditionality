# Section 4.2: Findings on Mission Creep

In 1985, none of the IMF programmes in the sample covered seven or more of the thirteen available policy areas. By 2019, 61.9% of programmes met this threshold. Coverage of a majority of the available policy space serves as the most accessible summary indicator of mission creep and provides the rationale for the subsequent analysis.

However, the raw threshold trend obscures an important detail. Between 2000 and 2005, which includes the implementation of the 2002 Conditionality Guidelines, the share of broad programmes increased from 56.5% to 59.2%. The intended narrowing of IMF conditionality is not evident in the unconditional descriptive data. Rather than representing a paradox, this pattern indicates a methodological issue: any reform effect is confounded by concurrent changes in programme type, programme size, and borrower composition. Isolating the reform's impact requires the regression framework outlined in Section 3.4.

Prior to examining the regression framework, the evidence on category composition merits separate consideration. GOVERNANCE conditions, which include institutional reform, environmental policy, and residual structural measures, accounted for 3.9% of all conditions in the IMF Monitor training data (1980–2019). In the MONA 2020–2024 sample, BERT assigns 13.2% of conditions to GOVERNANCE, while the independent descriptor-based lookup assigns 14.4%. Both classification methods indicate that the share of GOVERNANCE conditions has approximately tripled. The proportion of non-core conditions increased from about 10% in the 1980s to over 40% by 2019 (fig_11_noncore_share_1980_2024.png), and their absolute count rose from roughly 119 per year in the 1980s to approximately 1,388 per year in the post-2002 2000s, despite a decline in total condition volume (fig_09_absolute_noncore_counts.png). Streamlining reduced the core conditions but did not affect the periphery.

The primary regression, M3_extended, estimates the program-year scope trend while controlling for arrangement type, program size, loan size, region, transition economy status, repeat borrower status, borrower income, and financial crisis exposure. The principal predictor, year_c, is a continuous variable defined as calendar year minus 1990, centered to set the intercept at 1990. Its coefficient is an IRR of 1.010 (p < 0.001), indicating that, after controlling for program volume, each additional year is associated with a 1.0% increase in the number of distinct policy areas. This specification ensures that the year_c coefficient reflects scope breadth after holding log_n_conditions constant, thereby capturing broader reach rather than an increase in the number of conditions within the same areas. Over the 40-year period, a 1.0% annual compound rate results in an approximate 49% increase in scope breadth.

The 2002 Conditionality Guidelines resulted in a statistically significant reduction in scope: post_2002 is associated with an IRR of 0.912 (p < 0.001), indicating that programs after 2002 covered approximately 8.8% fewer distinct policy areas, controlling for all other variables. This represents a substantive reform response, but constitutes a level correction rather than a change in trajectory. The underlying year_c trend persisted at the same rate after 2002; within approximately nine years, cumulative scope growth offset the one-time reduction, and by 2019, programmes exceeded the pre-2002 trend line. Barnett and Finnemore (1999) anticipated this pattern, arguing that organisational reforms respond to external criticism by temporarily adjusting outputs while the underlying institutional logic of expansion remains. The 2002 result provides direct empirical support for this mechanism.

The 2009 lending overhaul, which eliminated Structural Performance Criteria and refocused conditionality on headline fiscal targets, did not significantly affect scope breadth: post_2009 is associated with an IRR of 0.999 (p = 0.885). This finding does not imply that the reform was entirely ineffective. Rather, it addresses the specific question of whether programmes after 2009 covered fewer distinct policy domains, to which the answer is negative. The reform's documented impact was on the type of conditions, not on the breadth of coverage. As shown in fig_03_condition_type_over_time.png, SPCs were removed from the data after 2009, consistent with the reform's objectives. Both findings are accurate and mutually consistent.

An interaction test demonstrates that neither reform altered the rate of scope growth. A model incorporating year_c × post_2002 and year_c × post_2009 interaction terms (M4) does not improve model fit relative to the primary specification; the likelihood ratio test yields χ² = 0.91 with two degrees of freedom (p = 0.634). The reforms adjusted the level of scope but did not affect the underlying trajectory.

Two demand-side explanations for the scope trend are evaluated using GDP per capita and crisis controls. Neither variable is supported: log_gdp_pc has an IRR of 1.008 and any_crisis an IRR of 1.009, with neither result approaching statistical significance. This constitutes a substantive finding rather than a model limitation. The observed expansion in scope is not attributable to a shift toward poorer borrowers with more complex structural needs, nor to an increase in crisis-driven lending that would necessitate broader emergency conditionality. While Bird et al. (2024) reported no change in the quantity of conditions between 2000 and 2018 (approximately 40 per programme in each period), the present analysis demonstrates a substantial change in composition. These findings are compatible and complementary: the IMF maintained its structural reach across a broader range of policy domains while reducing the overall number of conditions imposed.

The primary results are robust across multiple model specifications. Substituting cluster-robust standard errors with country-level random effects (M6, glmmTMB) yields coefficients identical to three decimal places: year_c = 1.010, post_2002 = 0.913, post_2009 = 0.996. Excluding arrangement_type from the specification (M5) alters year_c by only 0.001, indicating that arrangement type does not account for the mission creep signal. The threshold finding is validated at the 6+ and 8+ thresholds, with consistent qualitative patterns across all three cutpoints. The 7+ threshold is reported as primary because it represents a majority of the available policy space; the full sensitivity table is provided in Appendix B. Structural break tests at 2002 and 2009 assess the more stringent hypothesis that the entire coefficient vector changed at each reform year; neither test is significant (p = 0.334 at 2002; p = 0.794 at 2009). Extending the sample to 1980–2024 by including MONA 2020–2024 (M3_full_extended) leaves year_c essentially unchanged at 1.013, while post_2009 shifts to 0.932, reflecting the unusually low scope of COVID-era rapid access facilities in 2020–2021 rather than a substantive post-2009 reform effect.
